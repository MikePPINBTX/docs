# Generate Your First Report

Once an inspection is marked complete, the Report Workspace in the web admin is where you review, refine, and publish the final report — and deliver it to your clients.

---

## Opening the Report Workspace

1. In the web admin, navigate to **Orders** → open the order → click the inspection.
2. Click **Report Workspace** (or navigate directly via **Inspections → [Inspection] → Report**).

---

## Report Workspace Overview

The Report Workspace is organized into tabs:

| Tab | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **Findings** | Review and edit all captured deficiencies, organized by section |
| **Narratives** | Edit individual finding narratives with rich text formatting |
| **AI Draft** | Batch-generate narratives for findings that have field notes but no narrative yet |
| **Summary** | Edit the executive summary section of the report |
| **Ratings** | View and adjust section-level ratings |
| **Preview** | Generate and view a PDF preview |
| **Publish** | Publish the report and trigger delivery to clients |

---

## Reviewing Findings

In the **Findings** tab, all captured deficiencies are listed by section and subsection.

For each finding you can:
- **Edit the caption** — update the short title
- **Edit the narrative** — open the rich text editor for full formatting
- **Move** — reassign a finding to a different section or subsection
- **Flag for Summary** — mark the finding to appear in the executive summary
- **Delete** — remove a finding from the report

---

## AI Draft Narratives

If your inspectors used the mobile app's **Field Note** feature (a brief on-site observation note), the AI Draft tool can batch-generate full CAR narratives from those field notes.

1. Go to the **AI Draft** tab.
2. Click **Draft All** to generate narratives for every finding that has a field note but no narrative.
3. Review each generated narrative — approve or edit as needed.
4. Narratives with only a caption (no field note) are excluded from batch drafting; write these manually.

Cost: 4 IT tokens per drafted finding.

---

## The Executive Summary

The **Summary** tab contains the executive summary that appears on the first content pages of the PDF report.

### Summary Sections
- **System Performance Opinion (SPO):** Auto-generated paragraph evaluating the overall condition of major systems (Structural, Electrical, Plumbing, HVAC, Roofing). The SPO synthesizes your deficiency ratings across all subsections of each system.
- **Summary Deficiencies:** The findings you flagged as "summary items" in the Findings tab appear here as a concise list.
- **Inspector Observations:** Optional free-text area for overall property impressions.
- **Additional Information / Preamble:** Configurable standard language (limitation of liability, standards disclosure, scope of inspection).

### Skipping the Preamble
If the client has already received the preamble language in the pre-inspection agreement, you can toggle **Skip Additional Info** to omit the preamble from this report.

---

## Generating a PDF Preview

1. Go to the **Preview** tab.
2. Click **Generate Preview**.
3. The PDF opens in a new browser tab — review it for layout, branding, page breaks, and content.

The preview is not stored permanently. Click **Generate Preview** again after making edits to refresh it.

---

## Cover Photo

The cover page of the report displays a property photo.

- On mobile: the inspector can designate any captured photo as the cover photo from the Review screen.
- On web: you can upload a cover photo directly from the Report Workspace (drag-and-drop the image onto the cover photo placeholder in the Preview tab).

---

## Publishing the Report

When the report is ready:

1. Go to the **Publish** tab.
2. Click **Publish Report**.
3. The system:
   - Generates the final signed PDF
   - Uploads it to Google Cloud Storage
   - Sets `reportStatus = 'published'` on the inspection record
   - Fires the `report.published` workflow event

The `report.published` event triggers your configured email delivery workflow — sending the full report to the client and (if configured) a separate agent-facing summary to the buyer's agent.

---

## Report Delivery

Report emails are sent according to your **Communications Workflow** settings:

| Recipient | Default content |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Client(s)** | Full PDF report + repair request instructions |
| **Buyer's Agent** | Summary-only version (without pricing, without repair request) |

> Delivery addresses and email templates are configured in **Settings → Communications → Workflow**.

---

## The Realtor Portal

After publishing, the buyer's agent receives a **magic link** to the Realtor Portal — a read-only, branded web page showing the inspection summary findings. The portal does not require the agent to create an account.

The portal link is valid for 90 days after report publication.
